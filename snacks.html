Snacks/Desserts

Seoul's Tasty Delights


Seoul is also known to have a variety of snacks and desserts that are enjoyed by many locals and tourists. Korean desserts and snacks are also a big part of the food scene in Seoul. There are many cafes and like street food serves to be quick, accessible, and affordable. Here are some of the most popular desserts and snacks sold in Seoul.

PATBINSU

Patbingsu literally means red beans with ice. This dessert is a shaved ice dessert with sweet toppings such as chopped fruit, condensed milk, fruit syrup, and Azuki beans. Many varieties
of patbingsu exists in contemporary culture, but the early forms of patbingsu consisted of
shaved ice and two or three ingredients, red bean paste, tteok, and ground nut powder.
Today, some popular flavors are: green tea, coffee, and yogurt. Patbingsu can be found at
most fast food restaurants, cafes, and bakeries in South Korea.

HOTTEOK

Hotteok is a variety of filled Korean pancake, and that is eaten throughout the year, but is
most popular during the winter season because of its warmth. The dough for hotteok is made
from wheat flour, water, milk, sugar, and yeast. Handful-sized balls of this stiff dough are
filled with a sweet mixture, which may contain brown sugar, honey, chopped peanuts, and cinnamon. Today, many variations have developed such as green tea, pink bokbunja, corn hotteok, and more.

BUNGEOPPANG

Bungeoppang, a crucian carp cake/bread, is the Korean name of a pastry similar to the
Japanese fish-shaped pastry taiyaki. The name simply comes from the fish-like shape and appearance of the pastry, and it does not contain any ingredients from its namesake fish
or any other fish. Bungeoppang is sold as a snack by open-air food vendors throughout Seoul, Korea. These are pretty much sold the same way as Hotteok. These are also considered to be
street food as you can find them around many street food vendors.

BBOPKI

Bbopki is a sweet Korean traditional candy made from melted caramel and baking soda. Lots
of Bbopki would engrave a shape inside, which adds a little more fun to eating the food; these shapes include hearts or stars. Bbopki can be found in most of the city and is a popular treat among young children.

GYERAN PPANG (EGG BREAD)

This snack comes from GYERAN meaning egg and PPANG meaning bread. This is a savory
cake with egg on the center. Gyeran Ppang is always served hot. The merchant of Gyeran
Ppang is easily discovered in every corner of the street, market, school or bus station.

HWEORI GAMJA

Hweori Gamja, although is a snack, it is well known to be sold as street food. These are just
like potato chips, but there are made from a whole potato and cut into a shape of a tornado.
They are fun to eat and can be seasoned with various of flavors such as garlic, honey
barbecue, sweet and sour, and so on.
